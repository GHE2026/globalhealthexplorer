Indigenous Health

Understanding Indigenous knowledge, cultures, and health systems is essential for building equity, respect, and stronger global health for all communities.

Learning Gateway

Indigenous Peoples represent diverse cultures, languages, and knowledge systems across the world. Their understanding of health is deeply connected to land, community, identity, and environment. Learning Indigenous Health helps us understand that global health must be rooted in respect, equity, and reconciliation.

Why This Is a Global Health Issue

Indigenous Peoples in many regions continue to experience health inequities due to historical and ongoing impacts of colonization, discrimination, and unequal access to health services. At the same time, Indigenous knowledge systems contribute significantly to environmental stewardship, wellbeing, and holistic approaches to health.

Global Health Snapshot

  • Indigenous Peoples live in more than 90 countries worldwide
  • They represent over 500 million people globally
  • Indigenous communities often face significant health inequities
  • Indigenous knowledge contributes to sustainability and health innovation
